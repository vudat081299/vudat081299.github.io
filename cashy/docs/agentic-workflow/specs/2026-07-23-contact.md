# Spec: Contact (danh bạ đối tác vay/mượn)

## 1. Problem & Why

Cashy today records a loan against a free-form counterparty (or nothing at all): the person you lend to or borrow from is not a real thing in the data, just text on a single loan. That makes the same person unrepeatable across loans, un-renameable in one place, and impossible to group by ("who do I owe / who owes me" cannot be answered because there is no stable person to aggregate on).

This feature introduces **Contact** as a first-class entity in the workspace — a person you transact with — with its own stable identity, so a loan (and, later, anything else) can reference *the same person* by identity rather than by a copied name. Renaming the person updates every place at once; the person is reused across many loans; and the person becomes the natural key for the per-counterparty rollups that the loan redesign will derive.

This is **slice A** of a three-slice programme (A Contact → B Loan redesign → C stats aggregation toggle). Slice A delivers only the Contact entity and its lifecycle; it deliberately does **not** wire loans to contacts (that is slice B) — it only guarantees a contact has a stable identity that other records can reference. No product PRD/Confluence brief exists; this spec is the source of the *what*.

## 3. Business Rules

> Brand-new feature, no prior `.feature` living docs → every rule is `Thêm mới`. Each rule is UI-agnostic (states the business outcome, not the widget) and carries its raw ✅/❌ examples for to-bdd.

- `BR-contact-001` — A contact must have a display **name** that is non-empty after trimming surrounding whitespace; the name is mandatory for creating or saving a contact.
  - ✅ Create a contact with name "Anh Minh" → the contact is saved with a system-generated identity
  - ❌ Create a contact with an empty name (or only spaces) → rejected; the contact is not saved and the missing name is reported
  - ✅ Save a contact after changing its name from "Anh Minh" to "Minh" → the new name is stored

- `BR-contact-002` — Contact names are **not required to be unique**: two or more contacts may share the same name, and the system neither blocks nor merges them on the basis of an identical name.
  - ✅ Create "Anh" and then create another "Anh" → both exist as separate contacts, each with its own identity
  - ✅ A workspace holding two contacts both named "Anh" is a valid state

- `BR-contact-003` — A contact has an **optional username** — a short handle used to tell same-named people apart. It may be present or absent; its absence is valid.
  - ✅ Create a contact with name "Anh Minh" and username "minh_hsbc" → both are stored
  - ✅ Create a contact with only a name and no username → stored; the username is simply absent
  - ✅ Two contacts named "Anh", one with username "anh_vcb" → the handle distinguishes them

- `BR-contact-004` — A contact can be **created at the moment a counterparty is being assigned** and becomes immediately available for use, so a user is never forced to pre-register a person before referring to them.
  - ✅ While assigning who a loan is with, create a new contact "Chị Hoa" → the contact is created and can be selected as the counterparty in the same flow
  - ✅ The same assignment can instead pick an already-existing contact by searching its name or username

- `BR-contact-005` — A contact's **identity is stable and immutable across edits**: renaming or otherwise editing a contact never changes its identity, so any record that references that identity keeps resolving to the same contact and reflects its current name.
  - ✅ A record references contact X, then X is renamed "Anh Minh" → "Minh" → the reference still resolves to X and now shows "Minh"
  - ✅ Editing a contact's username or name leaves its identity unchanged (no new contact is created)
  - ❌ There is no operation that reassigns an existing contact's identity to a different value

- `BR-contact-006` — A contact can be **archived and unarchived**. An archived contact is retained in full but excluded from active selection (it is not offered when choosing a counterparty); unarchiving returns it to active selection.
  - ✅ Archive contact "Old Landlord" → it no longer appears among selectable contacts, but its record is kept
  - ✅ Unarchive it → it is offered for selection again
  - ✅ An archived contact's stored name and identity remain intact while archived

- `BR-contact-007` — A contact that is **referenced by nothing** can be deleted permanently and is removed from the workspace.
  - ✅ Create a standalone contact that no record refers to, then delete it → it is gone permanently
  - ✅ After deleting an unreferenced contact, it no longer appears anywhere and its identity is not reused

- `BR-contact-008` — A contact that **is referenced by at least one record cannot be hard-deleted**; the destructive action on a referenced contact is **archival** instead, preserving the historical resolution of its name for the records that use it.
  - ✅ Attempt to delete a contact that a loan refers to → hard deletion is prevented; archiving is the offered alternative
  - ✅ Archive a referenced contact → the referencing records still resolve its name; it is dropped only from active selection
  - ❌ A referenced contact is never silently removed while records still point at its identity

- `BR-contact-009` — Contacts are part of the **exported workspace and are restored on import**, with each contact's identity preserved so existing references remain valid after a round-trip.
  - ✅ Export the workspace, clear it, then import the export → all contacts return with their original identities
  - ✅ After import, a record that referenced a contact still resolves to the same contact

- `BR-contact-010` — The **demo/sample dataset includes seed contacts**, so a freshly seeded workspace has example people available (for the loan demo that slice B will attach to them).
  - ✅ Seed a fresh workspace with the sample dataset → a small set of demo contacts is present
  - ✅ Seed a fresh workspace without the sample dataset → no demo contacts are created

## 4. Design Requirements / NFR + Compliance/Security

- `NFR-contact-001` (Localization) — Contact **name and username must accept full Vietnamese text** (all diacritics) and round-trip through storage, export, and import without corruption or normalization loss. (Cashy is a Vietnamese-first app; a garbled "Nguyễn" is a data-integrity defect.)

*(No performance or security NFRs beyond the workspace's existing local-persistence guarantees — the feature is fully client-side and adds no network surface.)*

## 5. Out of Scope

- **Wiring loans to contacts** — the loan's counterparty reference field and its schema migration are **slice B** (loan redesign). Slice A only guarantees contacts have referenceable, stable identities.
- **Per-contact derived balances / "who owes me" aggregate** — computed on the ledger in **slice B**; a Contact stores no money and no balance.
- **Stats aggregation toggle** — **slice C**.
- **Extra contact attributes** — phone, email, address, contact groups/tags, duplicate-merge, and photo avatars are not built unless a later slice needs them (YAGNI).
- **Contact visual identity** (icon/colour vs auto initial-avatar) — a presentation decision for `to-tech-design`, not a business rule.
